Flesh-Eating Disease (Necrotizing Fasciitis)

What is it?

Necrotizing fasciitis is a severe, rapidly progressing infection that destroys muscles, fat, and skin tissue.

Symptoms

  • Severe pain and tenderness
  • Red or swollen area of skin
  • Fever and chills
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Skin blisters or black spots

Causes

You can get flesh-eating disease when bacteria enter a wound, such as from an insect bite, a burn, or a cut.

Prevention

Wash your hands often. And always keep cuts, scrapes, burns, sores, and bites clean.
